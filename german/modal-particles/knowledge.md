# Modal Particles — Knowledge

*Source: Hammer's German Grammar and Usage, 5th Ed. (Martin Durrell), Chapter 10*

Modal particles (Modalpartikeln, also called Abtönungspartikeln) are words which express the **speaker's attitude** to what is being said. They alter the tone of what is being said and make sure that the speaker's intentions and attitudes are clearly understood. They can:

- Appeal for agreement
- Express surprise or annoyance
- Tone down a blunt question or statement
- Sound reassuring

They are less independent than adverbs of attitude and **cannot normally occur in first position in a main clause** (before the main verb). They typically cannot be negated when used as particles. Many have a completely different meaning when used as regular adverbs, adjectives, or conjunctions — those uses are noted below.

True modal particles relate to the clause as a whole. **Scalar/focus particles** (Gradpartikeln) like *sogar* focus attention on a particular word. Several particles in this chapter function in both ways (e.g. *auch*, *nur*, *erst*, *schon*).

---

## aber

**Core meaning:** Expresses a surprised reaction; converts a statement into an exclamation. The surprise is about the **degree** of a quality (how much/how extreme), not the kind.
**Typical contexts:** Statements (exclamatory), initial position in exclamations.

**Examples:**
- Das war aber eine Reise! — *That was quite a journey, wasn't it?*
- Der Film war aber gut! — *The film was good* (emphatic, surprised)
- Der Kaffee ist aber heiß! — *Oh! The coffee is hot*
- Das war aber auch eine Reise! — *That really was some journey!* (strengthened by *auch*)
- Hast du was dagegen? Aber nein! — *Have you any objection? Of course not!*
- Aber Kinder! Was habt ihr schon wieder gemacht? — *Now, now, children! What have you been doing?*
- Aber, aber! Was soll diese Aufregung? — *Oh now! What's all the excitement about?*

**Register:** Spoken/colloquial; the exclamatory use is very common in everyday speech.

**Notes:**
- *aber* vs. *ja*: *ja* expresses surprise at the **kind** (unexpected discovery), *aber* at the **degree** (more than expected). Compare: *Der Kaffee ist aber heiß* (hotter than expected) vs. *Der Kaffee ist ja heiß* (you had expected cold coffee).
- *aber* is very similar in force to *vielleicht* in exclamatory use: *Der Tee ist vielleicht heiß!* is close to *Der Tee ist aber heiß!*
- When used initially in exclamations, *aber* can sound scolding or reassuring depending on context.
- As a **coordinating conjunction** (= 'but'), *aber* is distinct from the particle use.
- Within a clause *aber* can express a contradiction (similar to 'though', 'but'): *Mein Freund kam aber nicht* — *My friend didn't come, though.*

## allerdings

**Core meaning:** Expresses a reservation or qualification about what has just been said. Corresponds to English 'admittedly', 'of course', 'to be sure', 'all the same'. Very similar to *freilich* (10.14).
**Typical contexts:** Within sentences (concessive qualifier); standalone answer to questions (strongly affirmative, often hinting at an unstated reservation).

**Examples:**
- Es ist ein gutes Buch, allerdings gefallen mir seine anderen etwas besser — *It's a good book. Even so, I like his others rather better.*
- Wir haben uns im Urlaub gut erholt, das Wetter war allerdings nicht sehr gut — *The holiday was a good rest for us. All the same, the weather wasn't very good.*
- Ich komme gern, allerdings muss ich zuerst der Rita Bescheid sagen — *I want to come, of course I'll have to tell Rita first.*
- Kennst du die Angelika? Allerdings! — *Do you know Angelika? Of course! (I know what she's like, too!)*
- Ist der Helmut schon da? Allerdings! — *Is Helmut here yet? Oh, yes! (and you should see who he's come with!)*

**Register:** Neutral to slightly formal; more common in writing than many other particles.

**Notes:**
- When used as a **standalone answer**, *allerdings* gives a strongly affirmative answer but often carries a hint of an unstated reservation — different from a plain *ja*.
- *freilich* is very similar; *freilich* lacks the hint of an unstated reservation that *allerdings* sometimes carries.

## also

**Core meaning:** Confirms something as the logical conclusion or consequence of what has just been said. Corresponds to English 'so', 'thus', 'then', 'well then'.
**Typical contexts:** Statements (logical consequence); isolation at start of utterance (resumptive/transitional); introduces questions or statements.

**Examples:**
- Du wirst mir also helfen können — *You're going to be able to help me, then.*
- Wann kommst du also genau? — *So, when are you coming precisely?*
- Sie meinen also, dass wir uns heute entscheiden müssen — *So you think we're going to have to make a decision today.*
- Also, jetzt müssen wir uns überlegen, wie wir dahinkommen — *Well then, now we've got to think about how we're going to get there.*
- Also, besuchst du uns morgen? — *So, are you going to come to see us tomorrow?*
- Also, gut! — *Well all right then!*
- Also, so was! — *Well I never!*

**Register:** Very common in spoken German; extremely frequent as a conversational filler/connector.

**Notes:**
- *also* is one of the most common words in spoken German for signalling logical connection or resuming a topic.
- NOT the same as English 'also' (= 'too/as well'), which is *auch* in German.

## auch

**Core meaning:** A multipurpose particle. As a **modal particle**: stresses reasons why something is or is not the case, asks for confirmation, reinforces commands. As a **focus particle**: means 'even'. As an **adverb**: means 'too', 'also', 'as well'.
**Typical contexts:** Statements (explanatory/causal); yes/no questions (expecting confirmation); w-questions (rhetorical); commands (reinforcing); before nouns (= 'even').

**Examples — modal particle in statements (stresses reason):**
- Gerhard sieht heute schlecht aus – Er ist (ja) auch lange krank gewesen — *Gerhard's not looking well today – Well, he's been ill for a long time* (stresses the reason)
- Jetzt möchte ich schlafen gehen – Es ist (ja) auch spät — *I'd like to go to bed now – Well, after all, it is late.*

**Examples — modal particle in yes/no questions (confirmation):**
- Kann ich mich auch darauf verlassen? — *I can rely on that, can't I?*
- Hast du auch die Rechnung bezahlt? — *You did pay the bill, didn't you?*
- Bist du auch glücklich mit ihm? — *You're happy with him, aren't you?*

**Examples — modal particle in w-questions (rhetorical):**
- Was kann man auch dazu sagen? — *Well, what can you say to that?*
- Wie konnte er auch so schnell abreisen?! — *How could he have left as quickly as that?!*

**Examples — modal particle in commands (reinforcing):**
- Bring mir eine Zeitung und vergiss es auch nicht! — *Bring me a paper and be sure you don't forget!*
- Sei auch schön brav! — *Be sure you behave!*

**Examples — focus particle (= 'even'):**
- Auch der beste Arzt hätte ihr nicht helfen können — *Even the best doctor wouldn't have been able to help her.*
- Und wenn auch! — *Even so, no matter.*

**Examples — adverb (= 'too', 'also', 'as well'):**
- Peter will auch mit — *Peter wants to come too.*
- In Potsdam sind wir auch gewesen — *We also went to Potsdam.*

**Combinations:**
- *auch nur* — 'even', 'as/so little/much as': *wenn ich auch nur zwei Freunde hätte* — *if I only had just two friends*
- *oder auch* — 'or else', 'or even': *Du kannst Birnen kaufen oder auch Pfirsiche*
- *auch nicht / auch kein / auch nichts* — 'nor', 'neither': *Ich habe nichts davon gewusst – Ich auch nicht*
- *auch* in concessive clauses (= 'ever'): *Wer es auch sein mag* — 'Whoever that may be'

**Register:** All registers; extremely common.

## bloß

**Core meaning:** Restrictive sense = 'only', 'simply', 'merely'. A less formal and slightly more emphatic alternative to *nur* (10.26). More characteristic of informal registers.
**Typical contexts:** Commands (warning/intensifying), wishes (subjunctive), questions (rhetorical/wondering), statements.

**Examples:**
- Stör mich bloß nicht bei der Arbeit! — *You'd better not disturb me while I'm working.*
- Wie spät ist es bloß? — *I wonder just what the time is?*
- Wenn er bloß bald käme! — *If only he would come soon!*
- Sie hatte bloß 100 Euro bei sich — *She only had 100 euros on her.*
- Sollen wir Tante Mia einladen? – Bloß nicht! — *Shall we invite aunt Mia? – No way!*

**Register:** Informal/colloquial.

**Notes:**
- Critical distinction: *bloß* is **never** used in the 'reassuring' sense of commands (that is *nur*'s domain). *Lass ihn bloß reden!* = threatening ('just let him speak — and you'll suffer the consequences'). *Lass ihn nur reden!* = reassuring ('just let him speak, do').
- *bloß* can also be an adjective meaning 'bare, mere' (*mit bloßen Händen*) — a distinct non-particle use.

## denn

**Core meaning:** As a modal particle, used **exclusively in questions**. Most often tones down the question, making it less blunt and more obliging. With a negative element, signals reproach. Can also turn w-questions rhetorical, or yes/no questions into exclamations of surprise.
**Typical contexts:** All question types (w-questions, yes/no questions).

**Examples — toning down:**
- Hast du denn die Renate gesehen? — *Tell me, have you seen Renate?*
- Wie bist du denn gekommen? — *Tell me, how did you get here?*
- Wie geht es dir denn? — *How are you then?*

**Examples — reproach (negative element present):**
- Hast du denn keinen Führerschein? — *Come on, haven't you got a driving licence?*
- Bist du denn blind? — *Come on now, are you blind?*
- Was ist denn hier los? — *What on earth's going on here?*

**Examples — rhetorical (negative answer expected):**
- Wer redet denn von nachgeben? — *Who's talking of giving in?* (nobody!)
- Was haben wir denn damit erreicht? — *And what have we achieved by that?* (nothing!)

**Examples — yes/no questions as exclamations:**
- Ist das Wetter denn nicht herrlich? — *How lovely the weather is!*
- So hat er denn die Stellung erhalten? — *So he did get the job!*

**Further uses:**
- *denn noch* — to recall a fact: *Wie heißt er denn noch?* — *What is his name again?* (similar to *doch gleich*)
- *es sei denn (dass)* — conjunction 'unless' (formal)
- *geschweige denn* — 'let alone': *Er wollte mir kein Geld leihen, geschweige denn schenken*
- *denn* as coordinating conjunction = 'for', 'because' (19.1.2)
- *denn* in formal German for *als* 'than': *mehr denn je* — 'more than ever'
- North German colloquial: *denn* for *dann* 'then' — non-standard

**Register:** Common in colloquial speech; in w-questions almost automatic.

**Notes:**
- In colloquial speech *denn* is often reduced to *'n* and suffixed to the verb: *Hast'n du die Renate gesehen? Wie bist'n du gekommen?*

## doch

**Core meaning:** Used to persuade the listener of the speaker's point of view. Typically expresses a contradiction or disagreement. Often corresponds to English 'though', 'surely', or a tag question. Persuasive force is stronger when *doch* is **stressed**.
**Typical contexts:** Statements (disagreement/contradiction/agreement); questions (expecting positive answer); commands (impatience, urgency, or encouragement); exclamations (surprise); wishes (Konjunktiv II); standalone reply (contradicting a negative).

**Examples — statements (stressed = clear contradiction; unstressed = appeals for confirmation):**
- Gestern hat es **doch** geschneit — *All the same, it did snow yesterday.* (stressed)
- Gestern hat es doch geschneit — *It snowed yesterday, didn't it?* (unstressed)
- Ich habe **doch** Recht gehabt — *All the same, I was right.* (stressed)
- Ich habe doch Recht gehabt — *I was right, wasn't I?* (unstressed)
- Ich habe ihm abgeraten, aber er hat es **doch** getan — *I advised him against it, but he did it all the same.*
- Du hast doch gesagt, dass du kommst — *You did say you were coming, didn't you?*
- Die Ampel zeigt doch rot — *But the lights are red.*

**Examples — questions (expecting positive answer):**
- Den Wagen kann ich mir doch morgen abholen? — *I can collect the car tomorrow, can't I?*
- Du kannst mir doch helfen, (oder)? — *You can help me, can't you?*

**Examples — commands (impatient/urgent or advisory/encouraging):**
- Reg dich doch nicht so auf! — *For heaven's sake, don't get so excited.*
- Bring den Wagen doch (endlich) in die Werkstatt! — *For goodness' sake, take the car to the garage.*
- Freu dich doch! — *Do cheer up.*
- Lassen Sie mich doch (mal) das Foto sehen! — *Why don't you just let me see the photograph?*
- Kommen Sie doch (ruhig) morgen vorbei! — *Why not drop by tomorrow?*

**Examples — w-questions (asking for confirmation or repetition of forgotten info):**
- Wie heißt doch euer Hund? — *What did you say your dog is called?*
- Wer war das doch (gleich)? — *Who was that again?*

**Examples — exclamations (surprise):**
- Wie winzig doch alles von hier oben aussieht! — *But how tiny everything looks from up here!*
- Du bist doch kein kleines Kind mehr! — *You're not a baby any more, you know!*
- Das ist doch die Höhe! — *That really is the limit!*

**Examples — wishes with Konjunktiv II:**
- Wenn er doch jetzt käme! — *If only he would come now!*
- Wäre ich doch zu Hause geblieben! — *If only I'd just stayed at home!*

**Examples — standalone reply (contradicts negative):**
- Bist du nicht zufrieden? Doch! — *Aren't you satisfied? Yes, I am.*
- Nein doch, du hast jetzt genug gegessen — *Certainly not, you've had enough to eat.*

**Register:** All registers; one of the most versatile and frequent particles.

**Notes:**
- **doch vs. ja** in statements appealing for agreement: *doch* implies the listener might have a different opinion; *ja* presupposes speaker and listener already agree. Compare: *Du könntest dir **ja** Karls Rad leihen* (we both know you can) vs. *Du könntest dir **doch** Karls Rad leihen* (you might have thought you couldn't).
- As a **conjunction** (= 'but'): *Sie wollten baden gehen, doch es hat geschneit* — distinct from the particle use.
- In literary German *doch* can begin a clause with verb-first order to explain a preceding statement.

## eben

**Core meaning:** As a modal particle, expresses a confirmation that something is unavoidably the case — often with resignation, the sense of an inescapable conclusion, or 'that's just how it is'. Corresponds to English 'just'.
**Typical contexts:** Statements (inescapable conclusion, resignation); commands (no real alternative).

**Examples — statements:**
- Das ist eben so — *But there, that's how it is.*
- Ich kann ihn nicht überreden. Er ist eben hartnäckig — *I can't convince him. He's just obstinate.*
- Er zeichnet ganz gut – Nun, er ist eben ein Künstler — *He draws quite well – Well, he is an artist.*
- Ich mache es, so gut ich eben kann — *I'll do it as well as I can (given the circumstances).*

**Examples — commands (no real alternative):**
- (Dann) bleib eben im Zug sitzen! — *Well, just stay on the train, then.*
- (Dann) fahr eben durch die Stadtmitte! — *Well, just drive through the town centre, then.*

**Further uses:**
- Focus particle = 'exactly', 'precisely', 'just': *Das wäre mir eben recht* — *That would be just what I'd like*; *– Eben!* (standalone) — *Precisely!*
- *nicht eben* = lessens force, ≈ *nicht gerade*: *Sie ist nicht eben fleißig* — *She's not exactly hard-working.*
- Time adverb = 'just (now)': *Wir sind eben (erst) angekommen.* (*gerade* is very similar.)
- Adjective = 'level', 'flat': *Die Straße ist hier nicht eben.*
- Combination *eben mal*: *Reich mir eben mal das Brot!* — *Just pass me the bread, would you?*

**Register:** Common in colloquial speech; the 'that's how it is' sense very typical of spoken German.

**Notes:**
- *halt* (10.17) is a South German alternative to *eben* in its particle senses; the two are now interchangeable across Germany.

## eh

**Core meaning:** Very similar in force to *ohnehin* or *sowieso* — 'anyway', 'in any case'. Used predominantly in colloquial South German (especially Austria and Bavaria), but increasingly widespread.
**Typical contexts:** Statements.

**Examples:**
- Wenn ich arbeite, brauche ich eh immer mehr zum Essen — *When I'm working I need more to eat anyway.*
- Das neue Magazin ist für Sie eh ein Muss — *The new magazine is in any case a must for someone like you.*

**Register:** Colloquial; originally South German, now used more widely.

**Notes:**
- *ohnehin* (10.27) is the more formal/standard alternative.
- *sowieso* (10.31) is the widespread informal alternative across all regions.

## eigentlich

**Core meaning:** Emphasises that something is **actually** the case, even if it appears otherwise. Often used to change the topic or to moderate a statement. Corresponds to English 'actually', 'really', 'basically', 'in actual fact'.
**Typical contexts:** Questions (toning down, making casual; probing for the real answer); statements (indicating what is actually the case, softening a refusal or contradiction).

**Examples — questions (toning down):**
- Sind Sie eigentlich dieses Jahr schon in Urlaub gewesen? — *Tell me, have you been on holiday yet this year?*
- Wohnt Eva eigentlich schon lange in Hameln? — *Has Eva actually been living a long time in Hamelin?*

**Examples — w-questions (probing for the real answer, 'at bottom'):**
- Wie heißt er eigentlich? — *What's his real name?*
- Warum besuchst du mich eigentlich? — *Why, basically, did you come to visit me?*

**Examples — statements (actually the case despite appearances):**
- Er scheint manchmal faul, aber er ist eigentlich sehr fleißig — *He appears lazy sometimes, but in actual fact he's very hard-working.*
- Ich wollte eigentlich zu Fuß gehen — *In actual fact, I did want to walk.*
- Wir haben eigentlich schon zu — *Well, actually, we're already closed* (hinting an exception might be possible)
- Das darf man hier eigentlich nicht — *Strictly speaking, that's not allowed here* (but possibly . . .)

**Register:** Common in all registers of spoken German; very natural and frequent.

**As an adjective:** *eigentlich* = 'real', 'actual', 'fundamental': *Was ist die eigentliche Ursache?*

**Notes:**
- In questions, *eigentlich* is relatively lightly stressed when toning down; more heavily stressed when probing for the real answer.
- Often used with *denn* to make a question sound casual and exploratory.

## einfach

**Core meaning:** Emphasises that alternative possibilities are excluded. Corresponds to English 'simply' or (especially in commands) 'just'.
**Typical contexts:** Statements, commands, exclamations; frequently combined with *doch* and/or *mal* in commands, with *ja* in exclamations.

**Examples:**
- Ich bin einfach weggegangen — *I simply walked away.*
- Ich werde ihm einfach sagen, dass es nicht möglich ist — *I'll simply tell him it's not possible.*
- Warum gehst du nicht einfach ins Bett? — *Why don't you simply go to bed?*
- Leg dich (doch) einfach hin! — *Why don't you just go and lie down?*
- Geh doch einfach mal zum Zahnarzt! — *Why not just simply go to the dentist?*
- Heute ist das Wetter (ja) einfach herrlich! — *The weather is simply lovely today!*

**Register:** Common in colloquial speech.

**Notes:**
- *einfach* works as both a true **adverb** and as a **modal particle**, and the meanings differ:
  - Stressed adverb = 'in a simple manner': *Sie macht es **einfach*** (uncomplicated way)
  - Unstressed particle = 'simply', 'just', 'without further ado': *Sie **macht** es einfach* (she's just doing it)
  - Compare: *Du musst **einfach** anfangen* (begin in a simple manner) vs. *Du musst einfach **anfangen*** (you simply have to begin)

## erst

**Core meaning:** As a **modal particle**: intensifying force ('really', 'really then'). As a **focus particle** (scalar): indicates less or fewer than expected — corresponds to English 'only', 'not until', 'not before'. As an **intensifier in wishes**.
**Typical contexts:** Statements and exclamations (intensifying); wishes (Konjunktiv II); time expressions; before numbers/quantities ('only').

**Examples — modal particle (intensifying):**
- Dann ging es erst (recht) los — *Then things really got going.*
- Das macht es erst recht schlimm — *That really does make it bad.*
- Sie hat schon Hunger, aber das Kind erst (recht)! — *She may be hungry, but it's the kid who's really hungry.*

**Examples — wishes:**
- Wäre er doch erst zu Hause! — *If only he were at home!*
- Wenn er (bloß) erst wieder arbeiten könnte! — *If only he could start work again!*

**Examples — focus particle 'only' (before number/quantity, more to follow):**
- Ich habe erst zehn Seiten geschrieben — *I've only written ten pages* (more to come)
- Er ist erst sieben Jahre alt — *He's only seven years old.*

**Examples — focus particle in time expressions ('not until', 'not before'):**
- Er kommt erst (am) Montag — *He's not coming till Monday / He's only coming on Monday.*
- Es ist erst acht Uhr — *It's only eight o'clock.*
- Ich kam erst im Sommer nach Heidelberg — *I didn't get to Heidelberg until the summer.*
- Erst im September ist es mir aufgefallen — *It was only in September that I noticed it.*

**Register:** All registers.

**Notes:**
- **Critical distinction: erst vs. nur** (both = 'only'): *erst* implies more are to follow / process is not yet complete ('as yet'); *nur* sets a clear final limit (that and no more). Compare: *Ich habe erst drei Briefe bekommen* (only received three, as yet — more expected) vs. *Ich habe nur drei Briefe bekommen* (only three — and that's all).
- In time expressions: *erst* = 'not before (that point)'; *nur* = 'on that one occasion only'. Compare: *Sie ist erst (am) Montag gekommen* (didn't come before Monday) vs. *Sie ist nur (am) Montag gekommen* (came on Monday, not on any other day).
- The **opposite** of *erst* as a focus particle is *schon* (10.30.5).
- This distinction is not always maintained in colloquial speech.

## etwa

**Core meaning:** As a modal particle in yes/no questions: implies something is undesirable and suggests the answer ought to be *Nein*. In negative sentences: intensifies the negation. In conditional sentences: stresses a possibility. As a scalar/focus particle: expresses approximation ('about') or a suggested example ('for example', 'perhaps').
**Typical contexts:** Yes/no questions (negative expectation); negative sentences; conditional clauses; before numbers (approximation); before nouns (suggested example/possibility).

**Examples — yes/no questions (undesirable, answer expected to be no):**
- Hast du die Zeitung etwa schon weggeworfen? — *You haven't thrown the paper away already, have you?*
- Ist das etwa dein Wagen? — *That's not your car, is it?*
- Habt ihr etwa geschlafen? — *Don't tell me you've been asleep!*
- Sie wollen doch nicht etwa nach Paderborn umziehen? — *You don't want to move to Paderborn, do you?*

**Examples — negative sentences (intensifying):**
- Sie müssen nicht etwa denken, dass ich ihn verteidigen will — *Now don't go and think I want to defend him.*
- Komm nicht etwa zu spät zum Flughafen! — *Make sure you don't get to the airport too late!*

**Examples — conditional sentences (stressing possibility):**
- Wenn der Zug etwa verspätet sein sollte, verpassen wir den Anschluss — *If the train should be delayed we'll miss our connection.*

**Examples — before a number (approximation):**
- Ich komme etwa um zwei — *I'll come at about two.*
- Es kostet etwa dreißig Euro — *It costs about thirty euros.*

**Examples — before nouns (suggestion/possibility/example):**
- Er hat viele Hobbys, (wie) etwa Reisen, Musik und Sport — *He has a lot of hobbies, for example travelling, music and sport.*
- Willst du etwa (am) Sonntag kommen? — *You're not thinking of coming on Sunday, are you?*

**Register:** All registers.

**Notes:**
- The sense of *etwa* in yes/no questions (negative expectation) is very close to *vielleicht* (10.34.2).

## freilich

**Core meaning:** Concessive sense — very similar to *allerdings* (10.2). Corresponds to English 'admittedly', 'all the same', 'of course'. Originally typically South German, now more widely used.
**Typical contexts:** Within clauses (concessive); standalone answer to questions (strongly affirmative, often with *ja*).

**Examples — within a clause:**
- Es scheint freilich nicht ganz so einfach zu sein — *Admittedly, it doesn't appear to be that simple.*
- Wir nehmen ihn mit, freilich muss er pünktlich am Treffpunkt sein — *We'll take him with us, even so he'll have to be at the meeting place on time.*

**Examples — standalone answer:**
- Kennst du die Angelika? – (Ja,) freilich (kenne ich sie)! — *Do you know Angelika? Of course (I know her)!*
- Kannst du auch alles besorgen? – (Ja,) freilich! — *Can you see to it all? – Certainly I can!*

**Register:** Somewhat formal/literary in written German; common in colloquial South German.

**Notes:**
- *freilich* lacks the hint of an unstated reservation that *allerdings* sometimes carries when used as a standalone answer.
- *freilich* can **never** mean 'freely' — that is *frei* in most contexts.

## gar

**Core meaning:** Intensifying particle. Most commonly intensifies a **negative** ('not at all', 'whatsoever'). Less commonly emphasises the following word and indicates surprise (= 'even') — mainly literary.
**Typical contexts:** Negative sentences; (literary) emphasis.

**Examples — intensifying negatives:**
- Sie hatte gar nicht gewusst, ob er abfahren wollte — *She hadn't even known whether he wanted to leave.*
- Ich habe doch heute gar keine Zeit — *I really haven't got any time at all today.*

**Examples — intensifying *so/zu* with adjective:**
- Du darfst das nicht gar so ernst nehmen — *You really mustn't take that quite so seriously.*
- Es waren gar zu viele Leute auf der Straße — *There were far too many people in the street.* (*allzu* is a more frequent alternative)

**Examples — literary 'even':**
- Eher würde ich einem Habicht oder gar Aasgeier eine Friedensbotschaft anvertrauen... (Grass) — *I would rather entrust a message of peace to a hawk or even a vulture.*

**Register:** *gar nicht/kein/nichts* is common in all registers; the 'even' sense is mainly literary/elevated.

**Notes:**
- *gar* as alternative to *überhaupt* in negatives is standard and very common.
- *sogar* is a more frequent modern alternative to *gar* in the 'even' sense.

---

## gleich

**Core meaning:** As a modal particle, used in **w-questions** to politely request the repetition of information the speaker has forgotten. Often used with *doch*.
**Typical contexts:** W-questions (asking to recall or repeat information).

**Examples:**
- Wie war Ihr Name (doch) gleich? — *What was your name again?*
- Was hast du gleich gesagt? — *What was it you said?*

**Register:** Colloquial.

**Notes:**
- As a **time adverb**: *gleich* means 'immediately', 'straight away': *Ich werde ihn gleich fragen* — *I'll ask him right away.*
- Also: 'at once', 'at the same time': *Er hat gleich zwei Hemden gekauft.*
- *doch gleich* reinforces the memory-jogging force.

---

## halt

**Core meaning:** An alternative to *eben* (10.8) in some senses. Expresses an inescapable conclusion or the lack of alternatives — 'just', 'there's nothing to be done about it'. Originally South German, now used much more widely.
**Typical contexts:** Statements and commands (inescapable conclusion, no alternative).

**Examples:**
- Da kann man halt nichts machen — *There's just nothing to be done.*
- Dann nimm halt die U-Bahn! — *Just take the underground, then!*

**Register:** Colloquial; increasingly common across all German-speaking areas.

**Notes:**
- *halt* and *eben* are interchangeable in these senses. *halt* was originally South German, *eben* more North German, but both are now general.

---

## immerhin

**Core meaning:** Indicates that something might not have come up to expectations, but is acceptable or worthwhile at a pinch. Corresponds to English 'all the same', 'even so', 'at least'.
**Typical contexts:** Statements (salvaging something positive from a disappointing situation); standalone response.

**Examples:**
- Du hast immerhin tausend Euro gewonnen — *All the same, you won a thousand euros.*
- Wir haben uns immerhin einen neuen Blu-ray-Player anschaffen können — *Even so, we were able to buy a new Blu-ray player.*
- Das Wetter im Urlaub war miserabel, aber wir hatten ein schönes Zimmer – (Na,) immerhin! — *The weather was lousy on holiday, but we did have a nice room. – Well, that was something, at least!*

**Register:** All registers.

---

## ja

**Core meaning:** As a modal particle in **statements**: appeals for agreement, insisting that what the speaker says is correct and that the listener already knows this. In **exclamations**: expresses surprise. In **commands**: intensifies (often with warning tone). As a **focus particle**: 'indeed', 'even', 'nay'. As the **affirmative particle**: simply 'yes'.
**Typical contexts:** Statements (shared knowledge/agreement); exclamations (surprise); commands (warning/insistence).

**Examples — statements (appealing for shared knowledge/agreement):**
- Wir haben ja gestern davon gesprochen — *We did talk about that yesterday (you know).*
- Ihr habt ja früher zwei Autos gehabt — *Of course, you used to have two cars.*
- Ich komme ja schon — *It's all right, I'm on my way.*

**Examples — exclamations (surprise at the nature of something):**
- Heute ist es ja kalt! — *Oh, it is cold today!*
- Er hat ja ein neues Auto! — *Why, he's got a new car!*
- Das ist ja unerhört! — *That really is the limit!*
- Da kommt ja der Arzt! — *Oh (good), here comes the doctor!*

**Examples — commands (warning, insistence):**
- Bleib ja hier! — *Be sure to stay here!*
- Geht ja nicht auf die Straße! — *Just don't go out onto the street!*
- Er soll **ja** nichts sagen — *He really must not say anything (or else).*

**Examples — focus particle ('indeed', 'even', 'nay'):**
- Es war ein Erfolg, ja ein Triumph — *It was a success, indeed a triumph.*
- Sie konnte die Aussage bestätigen, ja (sogar) beeiden — *She was able to confirm the testimony, even on oath.*

**Examples — affirmative particle; tag:**
- Kommst du morgen? – Ja! — *Are you coming tomorrow? Yes!*
- Es geht um acht los, ja? — *We're starting at eight, aren't we?*

**Register:** All registers; very common in spoken German.

**Notes:**
- **ja vs. doch** (both appeal for agreement): *ja* always **presupposes** that speaker and listener already agree; *doch* implies the listener might have a different opinion.
- **ja vs. aber/vielleicht** in exclamations: *ja* (and *doch*) express surprise that something is the case **at all** (surprise in kind); *aber* and *vielleicht* express surprise at the **degree** (how much/how extremely). *Die Milch ist **ja/doch** sauer!* = surprise it's sour at all; *Die Milch ist **aber/vielleicht** sauer!* = surprise at how sour.
- *nur* is an alternative to *ja* to intensify commands with a warning note.

---

## jedenfalls

**Core meaning:** Stresses the reason why something should be the case or why it is not as bad as it seems ('at least', 'at any rate'). In commands: indicates something should be done 'in any event', 'anyhow'.
**Typical contexts:** Statements (concessive salvaging); commands (in any case).

**Examples:**
- Vielleicht ist er krank, er sieht jedenfalls schlecht aus — *Perhaps he's ill, at least he doesn't look well.*
- Er ist nicht gekommen, aber er hat sich jedenfalls entschuldigt — *He didn't come, but at least he did apologise.*
- Bring jedenfalls deinen Badeanzug mit — *Bring your costume along anyhow.*

**Register:** All registers.

**Notes:**
- *wenigstens* or *zumindest* are alternatives in the 'at least' sense.
- *auf jeden Fall* and *auf alle Fälle* are phrasal alternatives.

---

## lediglich

**Core meaning:** Indicates a restriction or limit before another word. An emphatic alternative to *nur* in the sense of 'only', 'no more than'. Used mainly in **formal registers** and can sound stilted in colloquial speech.
**Typical contexts:** Formal statements (restrictive).

**Examples:**
- Er hat lediglich zwei Semester in Münster studiert — *He only studied two semesters in Münster.*
- Ich verlange lediglich mein Recht — *I am only asking for what's due to me.*

**Register:** Formal/written; stilted in colloquial speech.

**Notes:**
- *lediglich* is a more formal alternative to *nur* (10.26) and *bloß* (10.5).

---

## mal

**Core meaning:** Moderates the tone of a sentence, making it sound less blunt — especially in commands, requests and questions. Corresponds to English 'just' (though English 'just' is used far less than German *mal*).
**Typical contexts:** Commands (toning down, softening); requests; questions.

**Examples:**
- Lies den Brief mal durch! — *Just read the letter through (will you?)*
- Hol mal schnell den Feuerlöscher! — *Just quickly go and get the fire extinguisher.*
- Würden Sie mir bitte mal helfen? — *Could you just help me?*
- Hältst du mir mal die Tasche? — *Just hold my bag for me, will you?*
- Sieh mal her! Hör mal zu! Komm mal herüber! Sag mal!

**Combinations:**
- *eben mal* — softens further: *Reich mir eben mal das Brot!* — *Just pass me the bread, would you?*
- *doch mal* — more casual: *Nimm doch mal ein neues Blatt!* — *Why don't you get another piece of paper?*

**Register:** Very common in colloquial speech; almost automatic in commands.

**Notes:**
- *mal* is **not** simply a short form of *einmal* ('once'). Key fixed combinations with *einmal/mal*:
  - *noch (ein)mal* — 'once again': *Ich habe ihn noch (ein)mal gewarnt*
  - *nun (ein)mal* — 'just' (emphasises lack of alternatives, ≈ *eben/halt*): *Es wird nun (ein)mal lange dauern* — *It's just going to take a long time*
  - *nicht (ein)mal* — 'not even': *Er hat sie nicht (ein)mal gegrüßt* — *He didn't even say hello to her*

---

## man

**Core meaning:** A colloquial **North German** equivalent to *mal* in commands and requests — 'just', 'go ahead'.
**Typical contexts:** Commands (colloquial North German).

**Examples:**
- Geh du man vor! — *You just go ahead.*
- Seien Sie man bloß ruhig! (Fallada) — *Just keep calm.*

**Register:** Colloquial; predominantly North German.

**Notes:**
- Completely distinct from the pronoun *man* ('one', 'you', 'people').

---

## noch

**Core meaning:** Multipurpose particle. (1) Indicates something **additional** ('another', 'else', 'too', 'as well'). (2) Temporal: something continuing longer than expected ('still', 'yet'), or a point before which something happened/will happen. (3) In w-questions: jogs the speaker's memory. (4) With comparatives: 'even'. (5) With *weder*: 'nor'.
**Typical contexts:** Statements; time expressions; w-questions (memory-jogging).

**Examples — additional:**
- Er hat noch drei Stunden geschlafen — *He slept another three hours.*
- Ich trinke noch eine Tasse Kaffee — *I'll have another cup of coffee.*
- Wer war noch da? — *Who else was there?*
- Und es hat auch noch geregnet! — *And apart from that, it rained too.*

**Examples — temporal ('still', 'yet'):**
- Angela schläft (immer) noch — *Angela's still asleep.*
- Klaus ist (immer) noch nicht gekommen — *Klaus hasn't come yet.*
- Ich habe sie noch nie gesehen — *I've never seen her (yet).*

**Examples — temporal (event by a certain point):**
- Ich habe ihn noch vor zwei Tagen gesehen — *I saw him only two days ago.*
- Noch im Mai hat sie ihre Dissertation abgegeben — *She managed to hand her thesis in by the end of May.*
- Ich werde noch heute den Arzt anrufen — *I'll ring the doctor before tomorrow.*

**Examples — w-questions (jogging memory):**
- Wie hieß er noch? — *Oh now, what was his name?*
- Wann war das Spiel noch? — *Oh now, when was the game?*

**Further uses:**
- With comparatives = 'even': *Er ist noch größer als du* — *He is even bigger than you.*
- With *weder* = 'nor': *Er liest weder Bücher noch Zeitungen.*
- With *so* and adjective in concessive clauses: *Wenn sie (auch) noch so fleißig ist, wird sie die Prüfung doch nicht bestehen.*

**Register:** All registers; extremely common.

---

## nun

**Core meaning:** (1) In questions: signals dissatisfaction with a previous answer, insists on correct/complete information. (2) Signals the speaker considers the topic exhausted, often as a resumptive at the start of a sentence ('well'). (3) As a **time adverb**: 'now' (less definite than *jetzt*).
**Typical contexts:** Questions (pressing for answer); utterance-initial (resumptive 'well'); statements (time).

**Examples — questions (pressing):**
- Wann kommt der Zug nun an? — *When does this train get in, now?*
- Stimmt es nun, dass sie verheiratet ist? — *Now, is it really true that she's married?*
- *Nun?* — *Well?*; *Nun . . . und?* — *And then what?*

**Examples — resumptive 'well':**
- Nun, natürlich hat er die besten Erfahrungen — *Well, of course he's got the widest experience.*
- Nun, wir werden ja sehen — *Well, we shall see.*
- Nun, meinetwegen! — *All right then.*

**Examples — time adverb ('now'):**
- Nun wollen wir umkehren — *Now we'll turn back.*
- Geht es dir nun besser? — *Are you better now?*

**Register:** All registers.

**Notes:**
- *nun* as a time adverb is less definite than *jetzt* and less frequently used to refer to the present moment strictly.

---

## nur

**Core meaning:** As a **modal particle**: intensifying force — depending on context, can be threatening/warning or reassuring. As a **focus particle**: restrictive 'only'. A frequent alternative: *bloß* (10.5) in all uses **except** the reassuring sense.
**Typical contexts:** Commands (threatening/warning or reassuring); w-questions (making them urgent); wishes (Konjunktiv II); focus particle (restrictive 'only').

**Examples — modal particle in commands (threatening/warning):**
- Komm nur nicht zu spät! — *You'd better not be late!*
- Nimm dich nur in Acht! — *You'd better be careful!*
- Geh nur nicht in dieses Geschäft! — *Whatever you do, don't go into that shop.*
- Sehen Sie nur, was Sie gemacht haben! — *Just look what you've done!*

**Examples — modal particle in commands (reassuring; *bloß* cannot be used here):**
- Lass ihn nur reden! — *Just let him speak, do!*
- Kommen Sie nur herein! — *Do come in!*
- Hab nur keine Angst! — *Don't be afraid, will you!*
- Nur weiter! — *Just carry on! (It's all right so far)*

**Examples — modal particle used initially (commands/instructions with no finite verb):**
- Nur nicht so schnell laufen! — *Just don't run so fast!*
- Nur aufpassen! — *Just be sure to look out!*

**Examples — modal particle in w-questions (urgent/rhetorical):**
- Wie kann er nur so taktlos sein? — *How on earth can he be so tactless?*
- Was können wir nur tun, um ihr zu helfen? — *Whatever can we do to help her?*
- Wie siehst du nur wieder aus?! — *What on earth do you look like?!*

**Examples — modal particle in wishes (Konjunktiv II):**
- Wenn sie (doch) nur anrufen würde! — *If only she would ring up!*
- Hätte ich nur mehr Zeit! — *If only I had more time!*

**Examples — focus particle (restrictive 'only'):**
- Ich wollte nur Guten Tag sagen — *I only/just wanted to say Hello.*
- Er geht nur bei schönem Wetter spazieren — *He only goes for a walk when it's fine.*
- Man kann es nur dort kaufen — *It's only there you can buy it.*

**Register:** All registers; very common.

**Notes:**
- For distinction with *erst* (= 'only'): see *erst* entry and 10.12.2c-d above.
- *nur dass* = conjunction 'only' ('except that'): *Die Zimmer waren in Ordnung, nur dass die Duschen fehlten*
- *lediglich* (10.21) is a more formal alternative to *nur*.
- *bloß* (10.5) is slightly more emphatic and more informal; **but** *bloß* cannot be used in the reassuring command sense — only *nur* works there.

---

## ohnehin

**Core meaning:** Indicates something is correct **irrespective of any other reasons given or implied**. Corresponds to English 'anyway', 'in any case'. More formal alternative to *eh* (10.9) and *sowieso* (10.31).
**Typical contexts:** Statements.

**Examples:**
- Er trinkt ohnehin zu viel — *He drinks too much anyway.*
- Der Zug hat ohnehin Verspätung — *The train's late anyway.*
- Du musst sofort zum Arzt – Ich hätte ihn ohnehin morgen besucht — *You'll have to go to the doctor right away – I would have gone to see him tomorrow in any case.*

**Register:** Neutral to slightly formal; more standard than *sowieso* or *eh*.

**Notes:**
- *sowieso* (10.31) is the more informal/colloquial equivalent (common everywhere).
- *eh* (10.9) is the Southern German colloquial equivalent.

---

## ruhig

**Core meaning:** Lends a **reassuring** tone to what the speaker is saying. Related to the adjective *ruhig* ('quiet', 'calm'). Used in commands (alternative to the reassuring use of *nur*) and in statements with a modal auxiliary.
**Typical contexts:** Commands (reassuring, 'go ahead', 'I don't mind'); statements with modal verbs.

**Examples:**
- Bleib ruhig sitzen! — *Don't get up for me.*
- Arbeite ruhig weiter! — *Just carry on* (don't let me disturb you).
- Auf dieser Straße kannst du ruhig etwas schneller fahren — *It's all right, you can go a bit faster on this road.*
- Sie dürfen ruhig hier im Zimmer bleiben — *You can stay here in this room, I don't mind.*

**Register:** Colloquial/spoken.

**Notes:**
- The meaning is clearly related to the adjective *ruhig* 'quiet, calm'.
- The reassuring sense of *nur* (10.26.1a-ii) is close to *ruhig*.

---

## schließlich

**Core meaning:** Indicates that the speaker **accepts the validity of a reason**. Usually corresponds to English 'after all'.
**Typical contexts:** Statements (accepting a reason).

**Examples:**
- Es liegt schließlich nicht genug Schnee auf der Piste — *After all, there's not enough snow on the piste.*
- Wir wollen ihn schließlich nicht zu sehr reizen — *We don't want to annoy him too much, after all.*
- Schließlich kann das einem jeden passieren — *After all, it can happen to anybody.*

**Register:** All registers.

**Notes:**
- As a regular adverb, *schließlich* also means 'finally', 'in the end', 'eventually' — a distinct non-particle use.

---

## schon

**Core meaning:** Has the **widest range of meaning** of all German modal particles. As a modal particle: (1) in statements, agreement or confirmation **in principle but with slight reservations**; (2) in future statements, **confidence** something will happen; (3) in questions, **persuasive force** with negative attitude; (4) in conditional sentences, **emphasises the condition**; (5) in commands, adds an **insistent note**. As a focus particle: (6) time — **sooner than expected** ('already'); (7) quantity — **more than expected**; (8) before nouns — **restriction**.
**Typical contexts:** Statements; future; questions; conditionals; commands; time expressions; quantities.

**Examples — modal particle, agreement with reservations:**
- Das ist schon möglich (aber . . .) — *That's quite possible (but . . .)*
- Ich wollte schon kommen — *Well, I did want to come.*
- Ja, ich glaube schon (aber . . .) — *Well, I think so (but . . .)*
- Niemand fährt über Ostern weg – Mutter schon! — *Nobody's going away – But mother is!* (corrective)

**Examples — modal particle, future confidence (reassuring or threatening):**
- Er wird uns schon helfen — *He'll help us all right.*
- Es wird schon gehen — *It'll be all right, don't worry.*
- Dem werde ich's schon zeigen! — *I'll show him all right!* (threatening)

**Examples — modal particle, questions (persuasive, negative attitude):**
- Wer kann diesem Angebot schon widerstehen? — *Who can refuse this offer?* (i.e. nobody)
- Na, und wenn schon? — *So what?*

**Examples — modal particle, conditionals (emphasising condition):**
- Wenn ich das schon mache, dann muss ich über alle Probleme informiert sein — *If I am going to do it, I'll need to know everything.*
- Wenn du schon ein neues Auto kaufst, dann aber kein so teures — *If you are going to buy a new car, then don't get such an expensive one.*

**Examples — modal particle, commands (insistent):**
- (Nun,) beeile dich schon! — *Do hurry up (then)!*
- Fang schon an! — *Do make a start!*

**Examples — focus particle, time (sooner than expected, 'already'):**
- Er war schon angekommen — *He had already arrived.*
- schon am nächsten Tag — *the very next day*
- Sind Sie schon einmal in Köln gewesen? — *Have you been to Cologne before?*
- Das habe ich schon 2005 geahnt — *I suspected that as early as 2005.*

**Examples — focus particle, quantity (more than expected):**
- Sie hat schon dreißig Mails bekommen — *She has already received thirty e-mails.*
- Er wartet schon eine Stunde auf dich — *He's already been waiting for you for an hour.*

**Examples — focus particle, before nouns (restriction):**
- Schon der Gedanke ist mir unsympathisch — *The very thought is repugnant to me.*
- Das geht schon daher nicht, weil . . . — *That's impossible, not least because . . .*

**Register:** All registers; extremely common.

**Notes:**
- *schon* has the **widest range** of all German modal particles — context and intonation are crucial.
- The **opposite** of *schon* as a focus particle (time/quantity, sooner/more than expected) is *erst* (10.12.2).
- Regional concessive alternatives to *schon*: *wohl* (North Germany), *zwar* (North Germany), *schon* itself (South/Central Germany).

---

## sowieso

**Core meaning:** Indicates something is correct irrespective of any other reasons given or implied. Corresponds to English 'anyway', 'in any case'. A rather more **informal** alternative to *ohnehin* (10.27).
**Typical contexts:** Statements.

**Examples:**
- Ich kann heute sowieso nicht arbeiten — *I can't work today anyway.*
- Der ist sowieso scharf auf sie — *He fancies her anyway.*
- Ich wäre sowieso nach Nürnberg gefahren — *I would have gone to Nuremberg in any case.*

**Register:** Informal/colloquial; common across all regions.

**Notes:**
- *ohnehin* (10.27) is the more formal/standard alternative.
- *eh* (10.9) is the Southern German colloquial equivalent.

---

## überhaupt

**Core meaning:** (1) Makes statements and commands more general ('at all', 'anyhow', 'in general'). (2) In questions, casts doubt on the basic assumption ('at all', 'in the first place'). (3) Intensifies a negative ('at all', 'not in the slightest').
**Typical contexts:** Statements (generalising/reinforcing); questions (casting doubt); negative sentences (intensifying).

**Examples — making statements more general:**
- Duisburg ist überhaupt eine grässliche Stadt — *Duisburg is a dreadful city anyhow.*
- Er liebte die italienische Sprache, ja die Sprachen überhaupt — *He loved the Italian language, indeed languages in general.*
- Ihr sollt überhaupt besser aufpassen! — *You ought anyway to pay more attention.*

**Examples — questions (casting doubt on basic assumption):**
- Er singt nicht besonders gut – Kann er denn überhaupt singen? — *He doesn't sing particularly well – Can he sing at all?*
- Wie konntest du überhaupt so was tun? — *How could you do such a thing at all?*
- Was will er denn überhaupt? — *What the dickens does he want?*

**Examples — intensifying negatives:**
- Du hättest es überhaupt nicht tun sollen — *You ought not to have done it at all.*
- Sie hat überhaupt keine Ahnung — *She's got no idea at all.*
- Ich weiß überhaupt nichts von seinen Plänen — *I don't know anything at all about his plans.*

**Register:** All registers.

**Notes:**
- *gar* (10.15) is a frequent alternative to *überhaupt* in intensifying negatives.

---

## übrigens

**Core meaning:** Introduces a **casual remark incidental to the main topic of conversation**. Corresponds to English 'by the way', 'incidentally'.
**Typical contexts:** Statements and questions (introducing an aside).

**Examples:**
- Ich habe übrigens erfahren, dass er eine neue Stelle bekommen hat — *Incidentally, I've found out he's got a new job.*
- Sie hat übrigens vollkommen Recht — *She's perfectly right, by the way.*
- Wo wollt ihr übrigens dieses Jahr hin? — *By the way, where are you going this year?*

**Register:** All registers; common in both spoken and written German.

**Notes:**
- *übrigens* can appear in **first position** before the verb (unlike true modal particles), reflecting its nature as an adverb of attitude as well.

---

## vielleicht

**Core meaning:** (1) In exclamations (unstressed): expresses surprise at the **degree** of a quality. (2) In yes/no questions: signals that the speaker expects a **negative answer** or finds the premise unacceptable. (3) As an **adverb of attitude**: 'perhaps', 'possibly'.
**Typical contexts:** Exclamations (surprise at degree); yes/no questions (negative expectation); statements/requests (politeness).

**Examples — exclamations (surprise at degree):**
- Die Kiste ist vielleicht schwer! — *How heavy the crate is!*
- Du bist vielleicht ein Idiot! — *You really are stupid!*
- Das hat vielleicht gegossen! — *It really did pour!*
- Ich habe vielleicht gestaunt! — *I wasn't half surprised!*

**Examples — yes/no questions (negative expectation):**
- Willst du mir vielleicht erzählen, dass . . .? — *You don't mean to tell me that . . ., do you?*
- Soll ich vielleicht bis 7 Uhr abends hier sitzen? — *I'm not supposed to sit here till seven at night, am I?*
- Arbeitet er vielleicht? — *Don't tell me he's working?*

**Examples — adverb of attitude ('perhaps'):**
- Sie ist vielleicht 30 Jahre alt — *She is perhaps thirty years old.*
- Sie wird vielleicht morgen kommen — *She may come tomorrow.*
- Könnten Sie mir vielleicht sagen, wo es zum Bahnhof geht? — *Could you perhaps tell me the way to the station?*
- Würden Sie mir vielleicht helfen? — *Would you perhaps help me?*

**Register:** All registers.

**Notes:**
- **vielleicht vs. ja/doch** in exclamations: *ja/doch* = surprise it's the case at all (kind); *aber/vielleicht* = surprise at the degree (how much). *Die Milch ist **ja/doch** sauer!* = surprise it's sour; *Die Milch ist **aber/vielleicht** sauer!* = surprise at how sour.
- Particle vs. adverb use is often only clear from **intonation**: the particle is unstressed. *Die Kiste ist vielleicht schwer* (adverb: perhaps heavy) vs. *Die **Kiste** ist vielleicht schwer!* (particle: how heavy!).
- Sense in yes/no questions is very close to *etwa* (10.13.1).

---

## wohl

**Core meaning:** (1) In statements: signals a **fair degree of probability** ('probably', 'I suppose', 'I imagine'). (2) In questions: signals **uncertainty** on the part of the speaker. (3) Stressed in statements: **concessive** sense ('admittedly', 'to be sure'). (4) In commands: makes them urgent, insistent, abrupt. (5) As a regular stressed **adverb**: 'well', 'fully'.
**Typical contexts:** Statements (probability; concession); questions (uncertainty); commands (insistent).

**Examples — statements (probability):**
- Das wird wohl der Briefträger sein — *That'll be the postman.*
- Sie sind wohl neu hier — *You're new here, aren't you?*
- Diese Probleme versteht er wohl nicht — *He probably doesn't understand these problems.*
- Ich habe ihn nie gesprochen, wohl aber oft gesehen — *I've never spoken to him, but I have often seen him.*

**Combinations:**
- *ja wohl* — more certain: *Sie wird ja wohl noch in Potsdam sein* — *She's pretty certainly still in Potsdam.*
- *wohl doch / doch wohl* — less certain, but hoped: *Er hat wohl doch noch einen Schlüssel* — *Surely he's got another key, hasn't he?*

**Examples — questions (uncertainty):**
- Wer hat den Brief wohl geschrieben? — *Who can possibly have written the letter?*
- Wie spät ist es wohl? — *I wonder what time it is.*
- Darf ich wohl bei Ihnen telefonieren? — *Might I use your telephone?*

**Examples — statements (concessive, stressed):**
- Er ist wohl mein Freund, aber ich kann ihm nicht helfen — *He may be my friend, but I can't help him.*
- Das ist wohl möglich(, aber . . .) — *That may be possible(, but . . .)*

**Examples — commands (insistent, urgent, abrupt):**
- Hebst du wohl das Buch wieder auf! — *Pick that book up again right away!*
- Wirst du wohl sofort wieder ins Bett gehen! — *Will you go straight back to bed!*
- Wollt ihr wohl endlich still sein! — *Once and for all, will you be quiet!*

**Examples — adverb ('well', 'fully'):**
- Ich fühle mich wohl — *I feel well.*
- Er weiß sehr wohl, dass er Unrecht hat — *He knows full well that he's wrong.*
- Schlaf wohl! / Leb wohl! — *Sleep well! / Farewell!*

**Register:** All registers.

**Notes:**
- *wohl* is closely related in meaning to the future tense used for probability (14.4.3) and often used together with it.
- *zwar* (North Germany) and *schon* (South/Central Germany) are alternatives to *wohl* in the concessive sense.

---

## zwar

**Core meaning:** (1) **Concessive**: typically followed by *aber* (explicit or implied) — the combination *zwar . . . aber* has the force of English '(al)though', 'admittedly . . . but'. (2) ***Und zwar*** — specifies what has just been mentioned: 'namely', 'that is to say', 'in actual fact'.
**Typical contexts:** Statements (concessive); specifying/elaborating with *und zwar*.

**Examples — concessive (zwar . . . aber):**
- Er ist zwar krank, aber er kommt heute Abend noch mit — *Although he's ill, he's still coming with us tonight.*

**Examples — und zwar (specifying):**
- Mein Entschluss fiel auf dem neuen Flugplatz in Mexico-City, und zwar im letzten Augenblick — *My decision was taken at the new airport in Mexico City, (in actual fact) at the very last minute.*
- Ich habe die wichtigsten Museen besucht, und zwar das kunsthistorische, das naturhistorische und die Albertina — *I visited the most important museums, namely the Museum of Art History, the Museum of Natural History and the Albertina.*

**Register:** All registers; *und zwar* is common in both spoken and written German.

**Notes:**
- In **North Germany**, *wohl* is a possible alternative to *zwar* in the concessive sense.
- In **South and Central Germany**, *schon* is used in the concessive sense instead.
- *zwar* without a following *aber* is possible but less common.
